Dogs of the Philippines

Philippine Forest Dog

The Asong Gubat, or Philippine Forest Dog, has a deep-rooted history in the Philippines, originating from ancient times when early settlers brought them to the archipelago. They were essential companions to indigenous peoples, serving as loyal companions, guardians, and skilled hunters in the rugged forests. Throughout history, the Asong Gubat has been celebrated for its intelligence, loyalty, and connection to nature. Revered in folklore and oral traditions, they embody the spirit of the Philippine wilderness. Despite facing challenges like habitat loss and crossbreeding, efforts to preserve the breed have gained momentum. Today, the Asong Gubat symbolizes resilience and cultural identity, cherished by Filipinos as a testament to the enduring bond between humans and animals.

Size

Asong Gubat is typically a medium-sized dog breed. They are sturdy and well-built, with balanced proportions suitable for navigating rugged terrain.

Height

The height of an Asong Gubat can vary, but they generally stand between 16 inches (40 cm) to 22 inches (55 cm) at the shoulder.

Weight

Asong Gubat usually weighs between 25 pounds (11 kg) to 45 pounds (20 kg). However, their weight may vary based on factors such as diet, exercise, and individual genetics.

Coat

Asong Gubat often have short to medium-length coats that are dense, weather-resistant, and well-suited to the forest environment. Their coat colors may vary and can include shades of brown, black, brindle, and tan.

Energy

Asong Gubat are highly active and agile dogs, well-adapted to life in the forest. They possess strong hunting instincts and excel in activities that require endurance, agility, and intelligence. As natural hunters and protectors, they are alert, vigilant, and make excellent watchdogs.

Average Lifespan

The average life span of an Asong Gubat is around 12 to 15 years, provided they receive proper care, nutrition, and veterinary attention. With their robust health and hardy constitution, they can thrive in diverse environments and conditions.
