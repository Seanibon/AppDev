Dogs of the Philippines

Aspin

Aspins, or Philippine native dogs, are an integral part of the country's heritage and biodiversity. With a diverse genetic background shaped by centuries of natural selection and human interaction, they exhibit remarkable adaptability and versatility. Believed to have descended from ancient pariah dogs, Aspins have served as skilled hunters, loyal companions, and cherished pets for generations. Renowned for their loyalty, intelligence, and unwavering devotion, Aspins have forged deep emotional bonds with Filipino families across diverse landscapes and cultures. They've played vital roles in society, from controlling vermin populations to protecting livestock and crops. Despite facing challenges such as neglect and the encroachment of foreign breeds, efforts to conserve and recognize Aspins as a distinct breed have gained momentum. Today, Aspins continue to thrive as beloved pets, embodying the enduring bond between humans and animals. With proper care and respect for their innate instincts, they stand as symbols of resilience, strength, and the enduring companionship that transcends time and cultural boundaries.

Size

Aspins exhibit a wide range of sizes, encompassing small, medium, and occasionally large individuals. Their size can vary depending on factors such as genetics, environmental conditions, and lineage.

Height

Typically standing between 38 to 63 centimeters at the shoulder, Aspins display a diverse range of heights.

Weight

Aspins possess a varied weight range, generally spanning from 9 to 23 kilograms.

Coat

Sporting a versatile coat, Aspins flaunt a spectrum of colors and patterns. Their short to medium-length fur is often dense and weather-resistant, providing adequate protection against the elements.

Energy

Aspins are characterized by their high energy levels and active disposition. Thriving on outdoor activities, they relish in running, playing, and exploring their surroundings. Regular exercise is essential to maintain their mental and physical well-being.

Average Lifespan

With proper care and attention, Aspins typically enjoy a lifespan of 12 to 15 years. However, some individuals may exceed this average, underscoring the breed's potential for longevity when provided with optimal care.
